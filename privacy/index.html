Privacy policy · Updated July 13, 2026

Privacy that respects the ride.

Rangecraft is designed to work without an account, advertising profile, analytics SDK, or cross-app tracking. This policy explains what stays on your iPhone and what an online mapping request can disclose.

✓

The short version

Bike and rider settings stay on your device. Place text and relevant coordinates leave the device only when you request an online search, route, jurisdiction lookup, or map. Rangecraft does not sell personal information or use it for advertising.

▣

Stored on your device

Bike specifications, rider preferences, optional age and safety-equipment answers, route preferences, saved-route state, and safety-notice acceptance are stored locally. Deleting Rangecraft removes this app-owned local data.

⌖

Sent for a requested result

A place search may contain the address or place text you enter. Route and jurisdiction requests contain the start, destination, or current coordinates needed to calculate the result. Normal network information, including an IP address, may reach the provider.

⊘

Not part of Rangecraft

The current app has no user account, advertising network, analytics SDK, data broker, or cross-app tracking system. It does not request access to contacts, photos, health records, or payment-card details.

☷

Your choices

You can deny location permission, enter places manually, use the included demonstration route without requesting a new route, change permission in iPhone Settings, or delete the app and its local profile data.

Online services

Who can receive a mapping request

The exact active services are shown inside Rangecraft under Profiles → Data & privacy. OpenFreeMap supplies the visible basemap, and the official U.S. Census Geocoder may be used for compatible U.S. street addresses. Public App Store builds do not use the community Nominatim, Photon, or Valhalla demo servers. A future release may use explicitly configured production search and routing providers, but this policy will be updated before that build is distributed.

Provider retention and security

Rangecraft uses encrypted HTTPS connections. A provider may process or retain request and network logs under its own policy for security, abuse prevention, or service operations. Rangecraft cannot delete data held independently by a third-party provider.

Location

Location access is optional and limited to while you use the app. Foreground guidance compares your position with the selected route on the device. A current coordinate is sent for routing only when you explicitly ask to rebuild from your current location. Rangecraft does not currently provide background navigation.

Children and optional age information

Rangecraft is not directed to children under 13. An optional rider age can be stored locally to identify possible e-bike age-rule conflicts; it is not used for advertising or profiling. A parent or guardian should supervise a minor's use and confirm every applicable rule.

This support website

This static site does not use Rangecraft analytics, advertising, accounts, forms, or cookies. GitHub Pages hosts the files and may process ordinary server and security information under the GitHub Privacy Statement.

Support correspondence

If you email Rangecraft, your email address, message, and attachments are sent to an iCloud Mail account and processed under Apple's Privacy Policy. Support correspondence is used to respond, investigate problems, prevent abuse, and maintain necessary records. You may request deletion of correspondence that Rangecraft is not required to retain by emailing RangecraftHelp@icloud.com.

Changes, deletion, and questions

The updated date above changes when this policy or the app's data practices change. Deleting the app deletes its app-owned local data. For privacy questions or corrections, email RangecraftHelp@icloud.com or visit the Rangecraft support page. Do not send a home address, precise ride history, or other unnecessary personal information.
