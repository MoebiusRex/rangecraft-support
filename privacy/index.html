Privacy policy · Updated July 14, 2026

Privacy that respects the ride.

Forthvane is designed to work without an account, advertising profile, analytics SDK, or cross-app tracking. This policy explains what stays on your iPhone and what an online mapping request can disclose.

✓

The short version

Rider profiles, bike profiles, their assignments, and any ride history you explicitly choose to record stay in the app on your device. Place text and relevant coordinates leave the device only when you ask for an online search, route, jurisdiction lookup, or map. Forthvane does not sell personal information or use it for advertising.

▣

Stored on your device

Bike specifications, rider preferences, optional age and safety-equipment answers, route preferences, the Safety and Use Acknowledgement record, and up to the 100 most recent opted-in ride records are stored locally. A ride can include start/end times, named endpoints, profile references, modeled battery and climb values, and a compacted GPS track with coordinates, timestamps, elevation, and accuracy. Forthvane has no app-sync service. The ride-history directory is excluded from device backup; other preferences may be included in an Apple-managed encrypted device backup under your iCloud or computer-backup settings. Deleting Forthvane removes this app-owned local data.

⌖

Sent for a requested result

A place search may contain the address or place text you enter. Route and jurisdiction requests contain the start, destination, or current coordinates needed to calculate the result. Normal network information, including an IP address, may reach the provider. The app uses a no-cookie network session without a disk response cache for these requests.

⊘

Not part of Forthvane

The current app has no user account, advertising network, analytics SDK, data broker, or cross-app tracking system. It does not request access to contacts, photos, health records, or payment-card details.

☷

Your choices

You can deny location permission, keep GPS recording off, enter places manually, use the demonstration route without a new route request, delete individual rides, clear all ride history, change permission in iPhone Settings, or delete the app and its local data.

Online services

Who can receive a mapping request

The exact active services are shown inside Forthvane under Profiles → Data & privacy. App Store builds use the official U.S. Census Geocoder for compatible street addresses, the U.S. Geological Survey GNIS service for named U.S. places, and Apple Maps for cycling directions and the Release basemap. Community demo search, map, and routing endpoints remain disabled unless replaced with approved production services. Development builds can use the public Photon, Nominatim, Valhalla, and OpenFreeMap services under their applicable policies.

Provider retention and security

Forthvane uses encrypted HTTPS connections. A provider may process or retain request and network logs under its own policy for security, abuse prevention, or service operations. Forthvane cannot delete data held independently by a third-party provider.

Location

Location access is optional and limited to while you use the app. Foreground guidance compares your position with the selected route on the device. A current coordinate is sent for routing only when you explicitly ask to rebuild from your current location. Forthvane does not currently provide background navigation. Saving a ride is off by default and starts only after you turn on “Save GPS ride to history” for that ride; the dashboard shows a REC indicator while points are stored. Ending the ride saves meaningful progress locally.

Sharing and export files

Text summaries and precise GPX files are separate, user-initiated choices in the iOS share sheet. Text can contain named endpoints. GPX can contain exact GPS or non-Apple route coordinates, timestamps, and elevation and may reveal a home, school, workplace, or routine. Apple Maps planned-route geometry is not stored or exported. GPX exports use a protected, unique temporary file excluded from backup; files older than one day are removed before a later export and iOS may remove them sooner. Forthvane does not receive a copy of anything you share.

Children and optional age information

Forthvane is not directed to children under 13. An optional rider age can be stored locally to identify possible e-bike age-rule conflicts; it is not used for advertising or profiling. A parent or guardian should supervise a minor's use and confirm every applicable rule.

This support website

This static site does not use Forthvane analytics, advertising, accounts, forms, or cookies. GitHub Pages hosts the files and may process ordinary server and security information under the GitHub Privacy Statement.

Support correspondence

If you email Forthvane, your email address, message, and attachments are sent to an iCloud Mail account and processed under Apple's Privacy Policy. Support correspondence is used to respond, investigate problems, prevent abuse, and maintain necessary records. You may request deletion of correspondence that Forthvane is not required to retain by emailing ForthvaneHelp@icloud.com.

Changes, deletion, and questions

The updated date above changes when this policy or the app's data practices change. Deleting the app deletes its app-owned local data. For privacy questions or corrections, email ForthvaneHelp@icloud.com or visit the Forthvane support page. Do not send a home address, precise ride history, or other unnecessary personal information.
