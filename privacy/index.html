Privacy policy · Updated July 13, 2026

Privacy that respects the ride.

Forthvane is designed to work without an account, advertising profile, analytics SDK, or cross-app tracking. This policy explains what stays on your iPhone and what an online mapping request can disclose.

✓

The short version

Bike and rider settings stay on your device. Place text and relevant coordinates leave the device only when you request an online search, route, jurisdiction lookup, or map. Forthvane does not sell personal information or use it for advertising.

▣

Stored on your device

Bike specifications, rider preferences, optional age and safety-equipment answers, route preferences, saved-route state, and the Safety and Use Acknowledgement record are stored locally. The acknowledgement record contains its revision, UTC completion time, app version, build number, and locale identifier; it is not transmitted or synchronized. Deleting Forthvane removes this app-owned local data.

⌖

Sent for a requested result

A place search may contain the address or place text you enter. Route and jurisdiction requests contain the start, destination, or current coordinates needed to calculate the result. Normal network information, including an IP address, may reach the provider.

⊘

Not part of Forthvane

The current app has no user account, advertising network, analytics SDK, data broker, or cross-app tracking system. It does not request access to contacts, photos, health records, or payment-card details.

☷

Your choices

You can deny location permission, enter places manually, use the included demonstration route without requesting a new route, change permission in iPhone Settings, or delete the app and its local profile data.

Online services

Who can receive a mapping request

The exact active services are shown inside Forthvane under Profiles → Data & privacy. OpenFreeMap supplies the visible basemap for open-data and demonstration routes. Apple Maps supplies live cycling directions and the Apple map used to display those directions when a separately approved open-data routing service is not configured. The official U.S. Census Geocoder may be used for compatible U.S. street addresses, and the U.S. Geological Survey GNIS service may be used for named U.S. places. Public App Store builds do not use the community Nominatim, Photon, or Valhalla demo servers. A future release may use explicitly configured production services, but this policy will be updated before that build is distributed.

Provider retention and security

Forthvane uses encrypted HTTPS connections. A provider may process or retain request and network logs under its own policy for security, abuse prevention, or service operations. Forthvane cannot delete data held independently by a third-party provider.

Location

Location access is optional and limited to while you use the app. Foreground guidance compares your position with the selected route on the device. A current coordinate is sent for routing only when you explicitly ask to rebuild from your current location. Forthvane does not currently provide background navigation.

Children and optional age information

Forthvane is not directed to children under 13. An optional rider age can be stored locally to identify possible e-bike age-rule conflicts; it is not used for advertising or profiling. A parent or guardian should supervise a minor's use and confirm every applicable rule.

This support website

This static site does not use Forthvane analytics, advertising, accounts, forms, or cookies. GitHub Pages hosts the files and may process ordinary server and security information under the GitHub Privacy Statement.

Support correspondence

If you email Forthvane, your email address, message, and attachments are sent to an iCloud Mail account and processed under Apple's Privacy Policy. Support correspondence is used to respond, investigate problems, prevent abuse, and maintain necessary records. You may request deletion of correspondence that Forthvane is not required to retain by emailing ForthvaneHelp@icloud.com.

Changes, deletion, and questions

The updated date above changes when this policy or the app's data practices change. Deleting the app deletes its app-owned local data. For privacy questions or corrections, email ForthvaneHelp@icloud.com or visit the Forthvane support page. Do not send a home address, precise ride history, or other unnecessary personal information.
