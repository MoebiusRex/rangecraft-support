Support for every mile.

Start with the quick checks below. Use only brief glances or designated one-tap controls while moving and only when conditions permit; stop safely for detailed or multi-step interaction.

!

Safety comes before the route

Posted signs, closures, official instructions, and current conditions always override Forthvane. Do not enter destinations, inspect route details, or troubleshoot while moving. Brief glances or designated one-tap controls are appropriate only when conditions permit; otherwise stop safely first. Never follow a cue that appears unsafe or unlawful.

⌁

A route will not build

Include a city and state with both places. Check the network connection and try a street address when a trail name is not found. The demonstration route remains available when production routing is not configured.

⚙

Range looks unexpected

Recheck battery capacity and health, motor efficiency, bike and cargo weight, assisted cutoff speed, rider weight, reserve target, and ride style. Wind, temperature, tire pressure, stops, and battery condition can still change real-world range.

⌖

Location or guidance is wrong

A brief glance or designated one-tap control is appropriate only when riding conditions permit. Stop safely before troubleshooting, confirm Location Services permission, and wait for a stable GPS position. Do not continue into a restricted or hazardous area. Use a posted detour or another trusted source.

Purchase or refund help

Apple processes App Store purchases and refund requests. Review the purchase in your Apple account or visit Apple's Report a Problem.

Contact

Report an issue or ask a question

Email is the best option for account, privacy, or other support that should not be posted publicly. Helpful reports include the iPhone model and iOS version, app version, approximate time, affected road or trail, what you expected, and what happened. Share only the location detail needed to investigate the problem.

Email ForthvaneHelp@icloud.com →
GitHub support requests are public. The public issue form is useful for non-sensitive, reproducible bugs. Remove home addresses, precise ride history, phone numbers, account information, and anything else you do not want visible online. Never send passwords, payment details, health records, or government identification by email or GitHub.

Optional: open a public GitHub support request or view the developer profile.

Map or route data corrections

Include a link to the location and reliable on-the-ground or official evidence. Forthvane can correct its own handling and help route an OpenStreetMap issue, but it cannot promise that every third-party dataset will update immediately.

Legal information corrections

Include the jurisdiction, official source link, effective date, and the exact statement that appears wrong. Forthvane's legal summaries are planning aids, not legal advice or permission. Local authorities and land managers remain the authoritative source.

Privacy questions

Read the privacy policy for local storage, online request providers, location use, and deletion choices. Email ForthvaneHelp@icloud.com for a privacy question or correction request without posting it publicly.
